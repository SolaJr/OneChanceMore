Glossophobia (the fear of public speaking) is the most common phobia high school and college students. This start up is combining modern psychology with Virtual Reality to help students conquor this fear.

Phobiapp is a multidispinary product that incoeprates Viruatl Reality technology with modern psychology to create an intuitive methods of training people to conquer a phobia of public speaking by using classical conditioning. When it comes to classical conditioning, there are two really important factors: the environment and the trigger.

By taking advantage of the immersive properties of a VR environment, we are able to simulate the process of having someone give a public speech to a large audience. Within the app settings, you can alter the environment to be similar to what you will experience when you are actually presenting on stage. You can choose between a classroom, theater, and auditorium so far in the beta.  

I developed a Unity Simulation of a person within the confines of each scenario while also developing a 2D interface to interact with. This interface will record data about the user such as how many times they say “um” or how many times they change their voices pitch. This will lead to a final evaluating score to measure the improvement of a participant. Now here is the cool part; after we get the final score, we won’t show it to them. We instead just provide compliments and suggestions for improvement. This process will prevent people from feeling like they are judged and opened to the idea of improvement.

The second part of this product is the trigger. The main reason similar apps exist but don’t work more than 40% of the time is that people will forget the skills they learned in the virtual world. Our initial idea for a trigger was to develop a blue tooth vest that would vibrate aggressively when the app reads a user has an increasing calculated error score and vibrate softly when a user has a relatively declining or constant score. Then if a user presents outside of the VR world with the vest and some vibrating, they will be classically trained to alter their behavior to be similar to the behavior they learned in the virtual world.
