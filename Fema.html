Federal Emergency Management Agency / Special Operations

Mitigation against one of the worst storms in Texas

Opportunity

Brief

December 2017 to January 2018

Position : Emergency Management Operations Intern (or Civil Engineering Intern)

Sponsor : Federal Emergency Management Agency (JFO Office in Austin)

Industries : User Research Assistant

Background : Hurricane Harvey is on record as one of the most destructive storms to hit Texas and our state continues its effort to recover from its impact.

Need Statement : Fifty engineering students will have an opportunity to receive FEMA training, practicum support, and serve as UX Researcers and Site Inspectors in areas affected by Hurricane Harvey (Houston, Galveston, and Corpus Christi). Site Inspectors support the Public Assistance Recovery process by conducting site inspections for damage sites listed on Site Inspection Work Orders and transferring the data to headquarters. Develop a system to improve the efficiency of this process.

 

My Contributions!

- While developing your program, work as a site inspector to support the Public Assistance Recovery process by conducting site inspections for damage sites listed on Site Inspection Work Orders

- Capture an Applicant’s claimed disaster-related damages in order to develop a detailed Damage Description and Dimensions report for Public Assistance claims

- Receiving and applied training on Emergency Management Operations and Site Inspector Fundamentals

- My program developed a spreadsheet with Visual Basic editor to improve data recording by over 30% by changing the tedious data entering process into a program that will find the data points needed for the report.
